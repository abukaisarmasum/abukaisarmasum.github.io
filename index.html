About

Abu Kaisar Mohammad Masum is a Ph.D. candidate in Quantum Machine Learning and Emerging Computing at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. He holds a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Daffodil International University, Bangladesh. He has authored multiple research papers in premier venues, including DAC, AAAI, DATE, ISLPED, TVLSI, Nature Portfolio, IEEE Embedded Systems Letters, ISCAS, QCE, ICCD, GLSVLSI, MWSCAS, AAAI Fall Symposium, ARQTEC @HPCA, and WUC@ASPLOS. In 2025, Abu was selected as a DAC Young Fellow 25. Most recently, he was a Winner of the NASA Beyond the Algorithm Challenge: Novel Computing Architectures for Flood Analysis, securing a $100K grant with his team for pioneering work that combines Quantum Computing and Hyperdimensional Computing (HDC) for real-world flood analysis.
